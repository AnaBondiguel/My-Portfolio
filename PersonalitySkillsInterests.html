Personality

Skills

Critical thinking: As a researcher, critical thinking skill is essential as we often receive criticisms for our research papers. I need to know what types of criticisms are useful for the future directions of our research, and how to use the criticisms to improve the research papers.

Problem-solving: As a leading author, I have to carry the majority of the work when we received comments from reviewers. Sometime their comments can be overwhelming and I have to stay calm and show positive energy to other authors. At the same time, I'm looking for solutions through discussions and trying different strategies. I often break down the problems and try to solve them piece by piece.

Communication skills: Research work is about exchanging opinions between authors. My colleagues and I always stay in touch on Zoom, Skype, and emails. An agreement between authors for the future research direction requires a clear explanation of both theoretical and empirical reasons. We often demonstrate our reasons and ideas by drawing diagrams and writing notes to keep things simple. I also often present our work at conferences and discuss the topic with other experts in the field.

Research and data analysis skills: Reading a large amount of literature and information online; collecting data by using survey and interviewing the top leaders and managers to identify common themes and making them connects with theories; being able to use software (Altas.ti & SAS/STAT) to analysis huge amount of data and produce data analysis reports. During this process, my communication and critical thinking skills are very important.

Publishing skills: Ability to construct a research paper with novel ideas or contributions to literature and strong arguments based on theoretical concepts and data results, good communications with editors of journals or books; ability to polish articles into a precise way that there is no gap between theories and empirical results; being able to revise research papers with editors/reviewers' comments from journals or books; retaining a positive attitude and being resilient when the papers get rejected. Teaching skills: Designing courses and learning materials; preparing my speech for the classes in order to deliver knowledge in a simple, clear, and interesting way (e.g. I always share my learning experience by drawing pictures or using stories to describe key concepts); getting feedback from students' questions and assignments, and making improvements for future classes.

Software skills: ATLAS.ti, SAS/STAT, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Canvas, MYOB, QuickBook, etc.

Accounting skills: Preparing financial statements, budgets, invoices to support the senior accountant and finance team

Mathematics, statistic & econometrics: with tertiary-level education certificates

Coding languages: HTML, CSS (or SCSS), (MORE in learning process)
